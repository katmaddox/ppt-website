Why buy a practice?

Acquire income, autonomy, and patients — day one

Buying an established practice means skipping the slow build-up of a startup. You inherit an existing patient base, trained staff, and proven revenue.

Immediate patient base

An established practice comes with hundreds or thousands of existing patient relationships — revenue from day one, no cold-start required.

Proven cash flow

You're buying audited financials, not projections. Our listings include trailing 12-month revenue, EBITDA, and collections data.

Equity you build

Practice financing

The right financing makes the right practice possible

Most practice acquisitions are financed — and the right lender can make all the difference. We work with a network of lenders who specialize in medical and dental practice loans, understand the nuances of goodwill valuation, and move quickly so you don't lose a deal to a slow underwrite.

SBA 7(a) loans up to $5M

The most common structure for practice acquisitions — low down payment, long repayment terms, and lender familiarity with practice cash flows.

Conventional practice loans

For buyers with strong financials, conventional options often close faster with fewer requirements than SBA programs.

Seller carry-back financing

In some transactions, sellers finance a portion of the purchase — bridging gaps and demonstrating confidence in the practice's ongoing success.
