Financial Disclosure Agreement — Dr. Priya Mehta

Practice Transition Partners
Financial Disclosure & Confidentiality Agreement

1. Purpose

This Financial Disclosure & Confidentiality Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between the Seller and the Buyer named above (collectively, the "Parties"), facilitated by Practice Transition Partners ("Broker"). The purpose of this Agreement is to authorize the release of detailed financial records pertaining to the practice listed for sale and to establish the terms under which such information may be used.

2. Authorized Financial Information

Upon execution of this Agreement by both Parties, Broker is authorized to release to Buyer the following categories of financial information ("Financial Information"): federal and state tax returns for the prior three (3) fiscal years; profit and loss statements; accounts receivable aging reports; overhead and expense summaries; production and collections reports; and any additional financial schedules prepared by or on behalf of Seller.

3. Restrictions on Use

Buyer agrees that the Financial Information is disclosed solely for the purpose of evaluating a potential purchase of the practice. Buyer shall not use the Financial Information for any other purpose, including but not limited to competitive analysis, solicitation of Seller's patients or employees, or disclosure to third parties other than Buyer's legal counsel, accountants, and lenders who are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

4. Seller Authorization

By signing below, Seller confirms that the Financial Information provided to Broker is accurate and complete to the best of Seller's knowledge, and that Seller consents to its release to the named Buyer under the terms of this Agreement. Seller acknowledges that Broker does not independently verify the accuracy of financial documents.

5. Return or Destruction of Information

If Buyer does not proceed with the purchase, Buyer shall promptly destroy or return all Financial Information and any copies or summaries thereof. Buyer shall confirm in writing to Broker that such destruction or return has occurred within ten (10) business days of either Party's decision not to proceed.

6. Term and Governing Law

The confidentiality obligations under this Agreement shall survive for a period of three (3) years from the date of execution. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California. Electronic signatures are considered legally binding originals.

By signing this Agreement, Seller authorizes the release of detailed financial records to the named Buyer exclusively for the purpose of evaluating a practice acquisition. Seller may revoke consent in writing at any time prior to closing if negotiations terminate.
